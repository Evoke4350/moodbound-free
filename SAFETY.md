# Safety Policy (Working Draft)

## Principles

1. Never present diagnosis.
2. Never present false certainty.
3. Always provide escalation options when risk indicators are high.
4. Use supportive, non-judgmental language.

## High-Risk Signals (implemented baseline)

- Rapid mood swings above configured threshold.
- Sustained severe depression or mania range.
- Combined low sleep and elevated mood risk patterns.

## Required UX Behaviors (implemented baseline)

- Show severity-labeled warning cards.
- Offer one-tap access to user-defined support contacts.
- Show emergency guidance language for highest severity.
- Preserve user agency while clearly stating concern.

## Prohibited UX Behaviors

- “You are definitely experiencing X.”
- “No concern” declarations without confidence disclosure.
- Dismissive language around user-reported distress.
